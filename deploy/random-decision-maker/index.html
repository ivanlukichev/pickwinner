What is a random decision maker?

A random decision maker is any tool that helps break a tie or choose between options without overthinking the process. It is useful when a group needs a fast answer, when you want to avoid bias, or when the result should feel neutral instead of personal. Common examples include heads-or-tails decisions, choosing who starts a game, picking a challenge, or selecting between activities.

Which decision tool should you use?

If there are only two outcomes, the Coin Flip is the cleanest choice. If there are many options, the Spin the Wheel tool creates a clearer visual decision moment. For game-style values, the Dice Roller works better. And if the decision depends on a range, the Random Number Generator is the right fit.

Where random decision makers are useful

These tools work well in classrooms, team activities, tabletop sessions, family games, meetings, and quick personal choices. They help groups move on without debate.
